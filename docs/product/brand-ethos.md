# LogYourBody Brand Ethos

This document is the canonical product and brand standard for LogYourBody. `AGENTS.md`, product specifications, design guidance, recommendations, marketing, and generated assets must follow it.

## Mission

LogYourBody helps adults improve changeable aspects of physical appearance through evidence-based body-composition tracking and sustainable fitness and nutrition decisions.

The product optimizes for aesthetics within health. Health, informed consent, user agency, and a sustainable relationship with body data are hard constraints, not tradeoffs.

Before building or publishing something, ask:

> Does this help the user understand or improve a changeable aspect of their appearance safely, measurably, and without wasted effort?

If not, it does not belong in the core product.

## What We Optimize

Prioritize outcomes the product can measure or support responsibly:

1. Reach and maintain a user-selected, healthy body-composition range.
2. Preserve lean mass while losing weight.
3. Build muscle in areas the user chooses to emphasize.
4. Improve proportions, posture, grooming, and photo presentation when they are in scope and supported by evidence.
5. Maintain results with the least necessary friction.

Useful outcomes include weight and body-fat trends, FFMI/FMI, lean mass, waist and other circumferences, ratios, progress photos, and sustainable rates of change. Always label whether a value is measured, estimated, interpolated, a population reference, or a user-owned target.

We do not optimize for exhaustion, punishment, athletic performance, entertainment, burning calories, earning food, or making a plan harder than necessary. LogYourBody is not a food logger or workout tracker.

## Individualization and Inclusion

- Start from the user's stated goals, current measurements, history, constraints, and preferences.
- Never assign an aesthetic goal solely from sex, gender, race, ethnicity, skin tone, hair or eye color, height, disability, or another immutable trait.
- Biological sex may be used only where it materially affects a validated physiological calculation or reference population. It is not a proxy for the user's aesthetic preferences.
- Muscle-priority examples are prompts, not templates. Do not assume that men want larger shoulders or women want larger glutes.
- Do not present one phenotype, body type, proportion, or demographic as universally attractive.
- Do not shame a current body, moralize food or weight, compare users against one another, or imply that attractiveness determines human worth.

## Safety Boundary

The app provides education and tracking, not diagnosis or medical care. It must not recommend unsafe rates, durations, or targets.

When the product knows a user is under 18, pregnant or postpartum, has a current or prior eating disorder, reports compulsive or distressing tracking, or proposes a target outside an evidence-supported healthy boundary, do not provide prescriptive aesthetic coaching. Offer neutral tracking, encourage a pause where appropriate, and direct the user to a qualified healthcare professional.

Never use streak loss, alerts, rankings, or emotionally loaded copy to pressure weight loss or body checking.

## Product Voice

The app should feel like a calm, world-class physique coach:

1. Here is what the data shows.
2. Here is the source and confidence.
3. Here is why it matters for your stated goal.
4. Here is the highest-ROI change.
5. Here is what to do next.

Be concise, specific, and honest about uncertainty. Never promise magic, perfection, guaranteed attractiveness, or a universally ideal body.

## Visual Identity and Photography

The visual system is premium, minimal, aspirational, cinematic, luxurious, and editorial. References such as Equinox, Alo, Lululemon, Apple, Tom Ford, Celine, and fitness editorials describe the level of restraint and polish; never copy their protected assets or distinctive trade dress.

Photography should feature diverse adults who look healthy, confident, well-groomed, stylish, and realistically attainable within their own phenotype. Favor composed editorial light, natural posture, and quiet confidence.

Avoid:

- racialized or phenotype-specific beauty ideals;
- exaggerated or drug-caricature physiques;
- sexual objectification or demeaning crops and poses;
- screaming, punishment, military, bootcamp, or excessively sweaty imagery;
- infomercial-style before-and-after presentation;
- retouching, generative edits, or perspective tricks that misrepresent proportions or attainable results.

Progress imagery must preserve dignity and make comparisons honest. Do not alter a user's body or imply that generated imagery is a measured outcome.

## Review Checklist

Before approving a product decision, recommendation, image, or piece of copy, confirm that it:

- advances a changeable, measurable, user-selected goal;
- follows the evidence standard;
- separates observations, estimates, references, and targets;
- respects the safety boundary and avoids medical claims;
- avoids immutable-trait ideals, stereotypes, shame, and coercion;
- fits the body-composition HUD scope and premium editorial system.

See [Evidence and Recommendation Standard](evidence-and-recommendation-standard.md) for research and recommendation requirements.
