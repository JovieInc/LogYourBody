# Evidence and Recommendation Standard

This standard governs product logic, educational content, AI or deterministic insights, marketing claims, and internal recommendations. It supplements the [LogYourBody Brand Ethos](brand-ethos.md).

## Evidence Hierarchy

Prefer the highest applicable level of evidence:

1. Systematic reviews and meta-analyses.
2. Consensus statements and position stands from recognized scientific or clinical bodies.
3. Peer-reviewed controlled trials and strong observational research.
4. Validated measurement standards and normative datasets.
5. Expert synthesis when stronger evidence is absent.
6. Coaching experience or mechanistic reasoning, clearly labeled as low confidence.

RP Strength and Menno Henselmans may be useful interpreters of research. They are not primary evidence and must not replace the underlying literature.

Do not use bro science, influencer popularity, testimonials, or a single attractive outcome as proof.

## Claim Requirements

Every material recommendation must record or make recoverable:

- the observed user data and its provenance;
- whether each value is measured, estimated, interpolated, or self-reported;
- the applicable population and outcome studied;
- the evidence source and publication date;
- the confidence level and important uncertainty;
- the user's stated goal and known constraints;
- the expected benefit, cost, and reason this is the highest-ROI next step.

Do not turn a population average or reference band into an individual prescription. Reference ranges describe context; targets belong to the user and may require clinician input.

## Recommendation Shape

Recommendations should follow this order:

1. **Observation:** what changed and over what period.
2. **Confidence:** source quality, missing data, and uncertainty.
3. **Meaning:** relevance to the user's selected aesthetic goal.
4. **Action:** one specific, proportionate next step.
5. **Review:** when to reassess or stop.

Prefer the smallest effective action. Do not recommend more effort merely because it feels more disciplined.

## Safety and Escalation

- Never diagnose, prescribe medication, or override a clinician.
- Do not recommend extreme restriction, dehydration, purging, drug use, or unsafe training.
- Suppress prescriptive aesthetic coaching in the elevated-risk contexts defined by the brand ethos.
- When evidence conflicts, say so and choose the safer reversible action.
- Re-review sources when a recommendation changes, when the population differs materially, or when relevant consensus changes.

## Language Rules

Use `reference range`, `estimated`, `measured`, `trend`, and `user-selected target` precisely.

Avoid `ideal`, `perfect`, `universally attractive`, `guaranteed`, `optimal for everyone`, and categorical claims unsupported by the cited evidence.
